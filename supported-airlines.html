Coverage

Airline coverage, explained simply.

RouteRefund is built around common U.S. airline manage-trip workflows. Coverage means we can intake the trip, review fare-drop eligibility, and guide the customer-approved next step. It never means automatic airline changes.

Best fit today

Booked direct with a major U.S. airline.

RouteRefund works best when the reservation was purchased directly from the airline, the fare can still be viewed in the airline account or confirmation flow, and the traveler is open to reviewed refund or flight-credit options if rules allow.

  • Airline confirmation or record locator is available
  • Passenger name and itinerary match the booked trip
  • No airline passwords, email access, or one-time codes are needed
  • Any cancel/rebook step is customer-approved first

Coverage means review, not autopilot changes

What RouteRefund can safely do with a supported airline.

Supported-airline coverage is about reliable intake and careful human review. Some airlines make credits or refunds easier than others, so RouteRefund keeps the workflow conservative until eligibility is clear.

1. Match the bookingUse the confirmation, passenger name, route, date, and fare details to avoid wrong-trip mistakes.
2. Compare like-for-likeCheck the same airline, dates, cabin, passenger count, timing, and practical rules before calling anything a savings option.
3. Ask before airline actionCustomer approval stays required before any cancellation, rebooking, or paid follow-up step.

Why confirmation details matter

Most record locators are short and not always airline-specific. A confirmation email or accurate passenger details help RouteRefund match the right reservation before monitoring starts.

What is not automated

RouteRefund does not automatically cancel, rebook, or change airline reservations. If a savings option looks eligible after review, the customer receives the next safe step before any airline action.
