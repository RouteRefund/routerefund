What happens next

Submit once. The system takes the first pass.

RouteRefund should feel simple: the customer gives the minimum trip details, automation tries to find the reservation, and fallback review catches the rest.

Step 1

Add details

Airline, confirmation, name, and DOB are used to start a secure lookup.

Step 2

Lookup runs

Supported airline reservations can fill route, date, and flight details automatically.

Step 3

Monitoring starts

Verified route/date details can feed owner-only Google Flights alerts and backup checks without exposing unverified savings.

Monitoring signals

How RouteRefund watches after the reservation is found.

Price-alert services are useful after a real route/date is verified. RouteRefund keeps those signals internal until a partner confirms eligibility and contacts you for approval.

Reservation lookup firstRouteRefund does not guess itinerary details from a confirmation code alone.
Google Flights alertsWhere supported, alerts use a RouteRefund tracking mailbox rather than your personal inbox.
Backup fare checksPaid/API checks stay partner-controlled and quota-safe; they are not run just because a page loads.
Human review gateExact savings and airline next steps stay private until evidence is reviewed and you approve any action.
