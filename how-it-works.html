How RouteRefund works

Book normally. Let RouteRefund watch after.

RouteRefund helps travelers monitor direct-booked flights for eligible savings after purchase, without handing over airline passwords or giving up control of the reservation.

Start trackingForwarding instructions

Built around control

  • Your airline reservation stays yours
  • No password, email inbox, or card-number sharing
  • Lower fares are reviewed before customer follow-up
  • No automatic canceling, changing, or rebooking
Simple setup

Three steps after you book direct.

Use the airline as usual, then let RouteRefund handle the monitoring busywork and tell you only when there is a reviewed next step.

1

Book with the airline

Buy direct so the reservation stays in your name and under your airline account control.

2

Forward or add the trip

Send the confirmation email to trips@routerefund.com or enter the trip basics manually in your private account.

3

RouteRefund watches

We monitor for eligible lower-fare signals and review the rules before presenting a customer action.

What to send

The handoff works best with one airline confirmation email.

RouteRefund needs enough booking detail to match the same flight later, not access to your airline or email account. If anything is missing, the trip stays in intake review until the next safe step is clear.

HelpfulAirline, confirmation code, passenger name, route, travel date, cabin, passenger count, and receipt amount when it is visible.
Never neededAirline passwords, email inbox access, one-time codes, security answers, card numbers, passport images, or loyalty-account recovery details.
Customer outcomeA private dashboard status, conservative eligibility review, and plain-language next steps before any airline action.
If a lower fare appears

Reviewed opportunities, not surprise itinerary changes.

Airline savings depend on route, dates, passenger match, cabin, timing, credits, and fare rules. RouteRefund checks those details before treating a signal as actionable.

CompareSame airline, route, date, cabin, passenger count, and practical itinerary fit.
VerifyFees, credits, restrictions, and timing are reviewed before exact savings are framed as final.
Ask firstYou receive clear next steps before any booking, cancellation, or rebooking action.
Good fit

Direct-booked U.S. airline trips.

  • You have a confirmation email or record locator
  • You booked with a supported airline instead of a bundled package
  • You want monitoring without checking fare calendars yourself
Handled carefully

Cases where we avoid overpromising.

  • Basic economy or highly restrictive fare rules
  • Third-party bookings where credits or changes are unclear
  • Any request that would require passwords or one-time codes
