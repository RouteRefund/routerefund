Trust & safety

Travel details are sensitive. RouteRefund is built around that.

The product should collect only what it needs, separate customer and owner access, and avoid direct card handling.

No card numbers in trip forms

Customers should not type raw credit card numbers into RouteRefund. Payments belong in secure Stripe-hosted flows.

Private customer dashboards

Supabase row-level security should ensure customers can only read and manage their own trips.

Restricted owner tools

Owner dashboards should only work for approved RouteRefund partner emails.

Data we may need

  • Passenger name and date of birth
  • Airline and confirmation code
  • Forwarded confirmation details
  • Price paid and operational notes

Data we avoid

  • Raw payment card numbers
  • Unnecessary personal details
  • Public exposure of trip records
  • Owner access from customer pages

Security work still matters

Before taking real customers, RouteRefund should verify Supabase policies, owner access, password reset flows, and Stripe payment flow. The website is moving toward launch-ready, but security verification is a required step.
