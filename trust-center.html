Trust & safety

Travel details are sensitive. RouteRefund treats them that way.

RouteRefund is built around a simple boundary: enough trip detail to monitor a direct-booked flight, never the credentials or payment data that would hand over your account.

Start trackingRead privacy policy

Customer-control promises

  • No airline passwords, inbox access, or one-time codes
  • No automatic canceling, changing, or rebooking
  • No credit card numbers in trip forms or notes
  • Human review before a savings next step is presented
Safety boundary

What RouteRefund collects versus what should stay private.

Clear intake rules help customers avoid oversharing and help RouteRefund review trips without taking control of airline accounts.

Helpful to shareAirline, confirmation code, passenger name, date of birth, route/date details, price paid, and a forwarded confirmation email.
Do not shareAirline passwords, email passwords, one-time codes, payment card numbers, passport images, or full government ID documents.
If unsurePause and contact support before sending anything sensitive. RouteRefund can explain whether a detail is actually needed.

Private customer dashboards

Customer trip pages are account-gated so travelers can review their own submitted bookings and monitoring status without public exposure.

Restricted partner tools

Partner operations stay separate from public customer pages and require approved partner accounts with two-factor authentication.

Secure payment path

Trip forms are designed for booking details, not raw card numbers. Any service fee belongs in a secure hosted payment flow.

Review workflow

Monitoring, review, payment, and airline action stay separate.

A possible lower fare is not treated as final until it has been checked against trip details and airline rules. This keeps the experience careful instead of rushing customers into risky changes.

1. IntakeRouteRefund verifies the booking details and avoids collecting credentials.
2. MonitorFare signals are watched without making airline reservation changes.
3. ReviewPassenger match, cabin, timing, fees, credits, and restrictions are checked.
4. Ask firstCustomers receive the next safe step before any booking or cancellation action.

Data we may need

  • Passenger name and date of birth
  • Airline and confirmation code
  • Forwarded confirmation details
  • Price paid and operational notes

Data we avoid

  • Raw payment card numbers
  • Passwords and verification codes
  • Public exposure of trip records
  • Partner access from customer pages
When something feels off

RouteRefund should feel boringly careful.

If a request ever seems to ask for too much access, stop and contact support. The normal RouteRefund flow does not require airline passwords, email inbox access, payment card numbers, or automatic permission to cancel and rebook.
