Trust & safety

Travel details are sensitive. RouteRefund is built around that.

RouteRefund collects only the trip details needed for monitoring, keeps customer and partner access separate, and avoids direct card handling.

No card numbers in trip forms

RouteRefund trip forms are designed for booking details, not raw payment card numbers. Any service fee belongs in a secure hosted payment flow.

Private customer dashboards

Customer trip pages are account-gated so travelers can review their own submitted bookings and monitoring status.

Restricted owner tools

Partner dashboards are kept separate from customer pages and require approved partner accounts with two-factor authentication.

Data we may need

  • Passenger name and date of birth
  • Airline and confirmation code
  • Forwarded confirmation details
  • Price paid and operational notes

Data we avoid

  • Raw payment card numbers
  • Unnecessary personal details
  • Public exposure of trip records
  • Owner access from customer pages

Customer-control principles

RouteRefund keeps monitoring, evidence review, payment, and airline action as separate steps. A possible lower fare is reviewed before customer outreach, and any airline booking, cancellation, or rebooking action requires separate customer approval.

Partner operations tools stay private and require approved partner accounts with two-factor authentication, while public pages avoid exposing partner-only workflows.
