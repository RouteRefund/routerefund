RouteRefund FAQ

Clear answers for travelers before they start tracking a booked flight.

What does RouteRefund do?

You book the flight. RouteRefund watches for eligible lower fares after purchase and helps capture money back or flight credit when there is a qualifying opportunity.

How do I add a trip?

Create an account, then either forward your airline confirmation email to trips@routerefund.com or enter the basic trip details manually.

Why do you need my airline, confirmation code, name, and birthday?

Airlines use those details to identify the correct booking. We collect the minimum practical information needed to verify and monitor the trip accurately.

Do you store credit card numbers?

No. RouteRefund trip forms should never ask for raw credit card numbers. When payments are needed, they should happen through secure Stripe-hosted links.

When does RouteRefund get paid?

RouteRefund is paid from savings we help capture. The key idea is simple: if there is nothing to save, there is nothing to collect.

Will every trip save money?

No. Airline prices and rules change constantly. RouteRefund cannot guarantee savings, but it can watch for opportunities customers would usually miss.

What happens if a lower fare appears?

RouteRefund reviews the fare, cabin, passenger match, timing, and airline rules before taking action under the authorization provided when the trip is added.
