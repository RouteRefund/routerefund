Getting started

What does RouteRefund do?

You book the flight. RouteRefund watches for eligible lower fares after purchase and helps capture money back or flight credit when there is a qualifying opportunity.

How do I add a trip?

Create an account, then either forward your airline confirmation email to trips@routerefund.com or enter the basic trip details manually.

Which airlines are a good fit?

RouteRefund focuses on major U.S. airlines and direct-booked reservations where policy, fare, and credit rules can be reviewed clearly. The supported-airlines page explains current fit by carrier before you add a trip.

What if I booked basic economy or a restrictive fare?

Some tickets have limited or no change/refund flexibility. You can still add the trip for review, but RouteRefund will treat restrictions conservatively and will not present an opportunity as eligible unless the rules support it.

Safety and privacy

Why do you need my airline, confirmation code, name, and birthday?

Airlines use those details to identify the correct booking. We collect the minimum practical information needed to verify and monitor the trip accurately.

Do you store credit card numbers?

No. RouteRefund trip forms should never ask for raw credit card numbers. When payments are needed, they should happen through secure Stripe-hosted links.

Do you need my airline password?

No. Do not send airline passwords, email passwords, one-time codes, or card numbers through RouteRefund forms or notes. If a future airline action is appropriate, RouteRefund will contact you with a separate customer-approved next step.

Will RouteRefund cancel or rebook without me?

No. RouteRefund monitoring is separate from airline booking control. We do not automatically cancel, rebook, or change reservations. If a reviewed savings option looks eligible, you receive the next safe step first.

Savings review and payment

When does RouteRefund get paid?

RouteRefund is paid from savings we help capture. The key idea is simple: if there is nothing to save, there is nothing to collect.

Will every trip save money?

No. Airline prices and rules change constantly. RouteRefund cannot guarantee savings, but it can watch for opportunities customers would usually miss.

What about airline fees or fare differences?

Fees, credits, refund rules, fare class, passenger match, and timing all matter. RouteRefund reviews those details before presenting an option as eligible, and exact savings may stay general until the review is complete.

What happens if a lower fare appears?

RouteRefund first reviews the fare, cabin, passenger match, timing, and airline rules. If an opportunity still looks eligible, we contact you with the next safe step before any airline booking, cancellation, or rebooking action happens.

Why do savings amounts sometimes stay general at first?

Early fare drops can disappear or fail an eligibility check. RouteRefund avoids presenting exact savings as final until the route, cabin, passenger, timing, and rule match has been reviewed.
